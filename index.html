Asian

Mild Coconut Chicken Curry

★ Batch

Freezes up to 3 months. Chili at the table for adults. Serve over rice.

Simple Home EditHalf Baked Harvest

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Cook the diced onion in a little oil until soft, then add garlic, curry powder, and tomato paste; stir 1 minute.
  2. Add the diced chicken thighs and brown on all sides.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and honey; simmer 15–20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce thickens.
  4. Stir in the peas or broccoli and cook 3–4 minutes. Season to taste.
  5. Serve over rice; add chili at the table for the adults.

20 minutes. Skip pepper flakes for the toddler batch. Beef keeps 4 days.

Damn DeliciousThe Recipe Critic

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Brown the minced beef in a dry pan, breaking it up; drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the garlic and grated ginger; cook 30 seconds.
  3. Stir in the soy sauce, brown sugar, and sesame oil (add pepper flakes only to the adult batch); simmer 2–3 minutes.

Tuna Mayo Kimbap

Eat same day. Fillings prep ahead and keep 2–3 days; rolling takes 10 minutes.

My Korean KitchenBeyond Kimchee

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Cook the rice, then toss it warm with a little sesame oil and a pinch of salt; let it cool slightly.
  2. Mix the drained tuna with mayo. Beat and fry the eggs into a thin omelette, then slice into strips. Julienne the carrot (sauté 1 minute) and cucumber.
  3. Lay a nori sheet shiny-side down on the mat and spread a thin, even layer of rice, leaving the far edge bare.
  4. Line the fillings across the center, then roll away from you with the mat, pressing firmly.
  5. Brush with sesame oil and slice with a sharp, wet knife. Eat the same day.

Honey Garlic Shrimp

10–20 minutes. Cook fresh — shrimp goes rubbery reheated. Pairs with rice + broccoli.

A Couple CooksSally's Baking Addiction

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Whisk the honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cornstarch into a sauce.
  2. Sear the shrimp in sesame oil, about 1–2 minutes per side, until pink.
  3. Add the garlic and cook 30 seconds.
  4. Pour in the sauce and toss 1 minute until glossy and thickened. Serve over rice with broccoli.

Chicken Tinga

★ Batch

Smoky shredded chicken for tacos, tostadas, or rice. Freezes well. Use ½ chipotle for toddler-mild.

Gimme Some OvenModern Proper (rotisserie)

Ingredients
How to make
  1. If not using rotisserie, poach the chicken until cooked through, then shred it.
  2. Blend the fire-roasted tomatoes, ½–1 chipotle, onion, garlic, oregano, and cumin into a smooth sauce.
  3. Sauté sliced onion, add the sauce, and reduce 5 minutes.
  4. Add the shredded chicken and broth; simmer 10 minutes until saucy. Pile onto tortillas or tostadas.

Carne Asada

Marinate 1–8 hours max. Works on a grill pan indoors. Slice against the grain.

Mexico in My KitchenSo Much Food

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Whisk the orange and lime juice, soy sauce, garlic, chopped cilantro, and cumin; marinate the steak 1–8 hours (no longer).
  2. Sear on a hot grill pan, 3–4 minutes per side, to your preferred doneness.
  3. Rest 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
  4. Serve in warm tortillas.

Fish Tacos (Tilapia or Cod)

20 minutes, pan-seared or baked. Mild fish, toddler-friendly without the sauce.

Isabel Eats (pan)A Couple Cooks (baked)

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Pat the fish dry and season with cumin, garlic powder, and chili powder (omit the chili for the toddler portion).
  2. Pan-sear 3–4 minutes per side, or bake at 200 °C / 400 °F for about 12 minutes, until it flakes.
  3. Stir lime juice into the sour cream or mayo, then toss with the shredded cabbage for a quick slaw.
  4. Fill warm tortillas with fish and slaw; finish with a squeeze of lime.

Moqueca (Brazilian Fish Stew)

★ Batch

Cod, tilapia, salmon, or shrimp in coconut-tomato broth. Batch the sauce; cook fish fresh in it.

RecipeTin EatsFeasting at Home

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Sauté the onion, bell peppers, and garlic until soft, then stir in the paprika.
  2. Add the tomatoes and coconut milk; simmer 10 minutes to build the sauce (batch this ahead if you like).
  3. Nestle the fish into the broth and simmer gently 8–10 minutes, until just cooked.
  4. Finish with lime juice and cilantro. Serve over rice.

High Protein Chocolate Brownie

★ Batch

A brownie base with a protein-cookie top. Makes 2 trays — freezer-friendly. Add more flour to the brownie batter for a denser bite.

Recipe by @nickkaz.fit

Per tray: 32 g protein · 32 g carbs · 5 g fat · 305 kcal

Ingredients

Cookie batter — wet

Cookie batter — dry

Brownie batter — wet

Brownie batter — dry

Topping

How to make
  1. Preheat oven to 180 °C / 350 °F and lightly spray two 5- or 6-inch springform pans.
  2. Mix the cookie batter wet and dry ingredients separately, then combine.
  3. Mix the brownie batter wet and dry ingredients separately, then combine. Do not overmix.
  4. Divide the brownie batter evenly between both pans.
  5. Dollop the cookie batter over the brownie batter, gently spread, and add the choc chips.
  6. Bake 28–38 min (6-inch) or 32–42 min (5-inch), until the edges are set and the centers are slightly soft.
  7. Cool in the pans for 30 minutes before removing.

Choc Banana Cake

★ Batch

One-bowl blender cake, makes 8 slices. Sweetened with banana and maple. Cool, then chill in the fridge before eating.

Recipe by @fillingmeals

Per slice (of 8): 7 g protein · 17 g carbs · 2 g fat · 100 kcal

Ingredients
How to make
  1. Preheat oven to 180 °C / 360 °F and line a baking tin.
  2. Put everything in a bowl — bananas, eggs, cottage cheese, cocoa, maple syrup, baking powder — and blend smooth with a stick blender (a whisk or regular blender works too).
  3. Pour into the tin and bake 45 minutes.
  4. Let it cool, then store in the fridge — it's best eaten chilled. Slice into 8.
